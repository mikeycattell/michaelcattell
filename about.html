Nice to meet you, I’m Michael. You can call me Michael, Mike or Mikey.

I am a designer, mentor, startup advisor and decent at impressions.

I’m Mikey Cattell, a designer, and recovering developer. I work with some of my very favourite people at the Department for Work and Pensions (a British government department responsible for welfare and pension policy) where I serve as their Senior Interaction Designer and make services and products easy to use for users, the picture clearer for stakeholders and things for the web.

I live and work in Leeds with my wife Emma, my daughter, and our little dog Ralph. I’m a fan of football (LA Galaxy!), craft beers, running (sometimes), grower of veg, game player, terrible jokes, serial cake testing, and listening to a lot of music and podcasts.

With over 15 years of experience being a curious designer in public and private sectors, agency and contracting roles for clients of all sizes, specialising in leading and facilitating design, designing clean interfaces, as well as plotting services and products from discovery to launch.

I do all of this by telling memorable stories to describe the work I am on - using strategy, research, visual design, and user-centered design approach to complex design problems.

I'm passionate about all of my work, giving my 100% in each project, totally committing to delivering a solution of a high standard.
